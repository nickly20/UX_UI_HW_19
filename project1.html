WORK RESUME ABOUT CONTACT

introduction/

The redesign of the NASA website is a project focused on enhancing user experience by implementing a modern and intuitive design. The goal is to improve the website's information hierarchy and navigation, with the aim of making it easier for visitors to find the information they need. This will be achieved through rigorous user research and the use of responsive design principles, ensuring that the site looks and functions well across all devices. Additionally, multimedia elements will be utilized to highlight NASA's groundbreaking accomplishments and enhance the overall user experience. This case study will detail the process and results of this exciting project.

challenge

NASA's current website suffers from a text-heavy, cluttered, and disorganized design, which makes it difficult for users to navigate and find relevant content. The website's interface lacks a clear hierarchy of information, resulting in a confusing user experience. The excessive use of text also makes it challenging for users to quickly scan and find what they need. This issue is compounded by the website's cluttered layout, which features an overwhelming amount of information on each page. As a result, users are likely to feel overwhelmed and frustrated by the website's design, leading to a poor user experience. A redesign of the NASA website is necessary to address these issues and create a more user-friendly and visually appealing platform for sharing NASA's work and achievements with the public.

solution

A solution for the NASA website redesign would be to implement new design elements such as a clean, modern layout with a clear information hierarchy, reduced text content, and improved navigation. This could be achieved by conducting user research to determine user needs and preferences and using a responsive design to optimize the website for various devices.

The ultimate goal would be to create a website that effectively showcases NASA's research, discoveries, and achievements to the public, while also making it easier for users to navigate and find the information they need. Additionally, a website with increased traffic could help NASA communicate its research and discoveries to a broader audience, promoting science, increasing public understanding and support for its work.

methodology

research | NASA

NASA, or the National Aeronautics and Space Administration, is a United States government agency responsible for the country's civilian space program, as well as for aeronautics and aerospace research. NASA was established in 1958, succeeding the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (NACA). Since its founding, NASA has been at the forefront of space exploration, conducting groundbreaking missions such as the Apollo moon landings and the Space Shuttle program. In addition to its space-related activities, NASA also conducts research in areas such as climate science, astrophysics, and aeronautics, with the goal of advancing scientific knowledge and benefiting society as a whole.

user research

We conducted a research study to gather feedback on the current design of our website. The study involved 10+ user interviews and 21 survey results who reviewed the website and provided their opinions on various aspects of the design.

goals

From our research, we wanted to know the following:
  • users’ likes, dislikes, expectations and overall experience of NASA’S current website.
  • Awareness about the information provided.
  • Understand how users interact with library services and information.
  • Understand users attitudes and behaviors with respect to NASA’s interface.

user interviews

We conducted 10 virtual interviews and found that the current website was too text-dense and busy, making it suffocating for users. The participants also expressed confusion with the hierarchy of the website and found it difficult to navigate.

Additionally, a heuristic evaluation found that the website's dark color scheme caused reader fatigue and made it harder to read. The participants also reported that the website lacked clear user direction, making it challenging to find the information they were looking for.

After conducting the interviews, we conducted a brainstorming session and analyzed all the data that we had gathered.

brainstorming & ideation

After completing our research and gaining a clear understanding of the issues users faced with the NASA website, we proceeded with a brainstorming session to generate potential solutions and layouts for the redesign.

feature prioritization

  • Clean and modern design: The website should have a clean and modern design that is visually appealing and easy to navigate.
  • Condensed Navigation bar: The nav bar contains an overwhelming amount of information and options, would benefit with condensed version.
  • Highlight latest scientific news and events: The homepage should highlight the latest scientific news and events, making it easy for users to stay up-to-date.
  • Easy to understand content: The content should be easy to understand and engaging for users of all levels of scientific knowledge.
  • Research section: The website should have a dedicated section for research, where users can search for specific data or scientific papers.
  • Efficient search function: The search function should be efficient, making it easy for users to find the information they need quickly.
  • Newsletter: The website should have a newsletter sign-up option, allowing users like Kevin to receive regular updates on the latest scientific news and discoveries.

Sitemap

To improve the user experience of the NASA website, we reorganized the site navigation into four main sections: Articles, Missions, Follow, and Contents. We consolidated all relevant items, with the goal of making the site more intuitive to navigate. We prioritized categories that we recognized as carrying the most traffic and outlined the major components of the site within these sections. Ultimately, this resulted in a more streamlined and user-friendly navigation experience.

A look at NASA’s present navigation system*

During the redesign process of the NASA website, our team experimented with several alternate sitemap orientations for the navigation bar. We considered various options that we believed could be more effective in organizing and presenting the site's content. However, after thorough evaluation and user testing, we found that nesting all components into four major categories titled "Articles", "Missions", "Follow", and "Content" was the most logical and user-friendly approach. This allowed us to consolidate all relevant items while making it easy for users to understand and navigate the site's content.

style guide

NASA explores the unknown in air and space, innovates for the benefit of humanity, and inspires the world through discovery. Design and science run parallel and as such NASA's website should better reflect the characteristics of innovation so synonymous with NASA as a whole. The UI dimension must demonstrate succinct animation, font, layout, and information display. Stylistically the UI must be clean, thorough, and technical while avoiding an esoteric nature that is inherit in astronomy. By making more dynamic animations, introducing inviting colors, and consolidating user paths the site will be made more palpable to the average visitor.

reflection

After identifying key features through prioritization and testing with users, the NASA website redesign successfully met its objectives of improving user experience and meeting the needs of its target audience. The new design includes a modern and visually appealing interface, simplified content, clear hierarchy, lighter color scheme, and a newsletter sign-up option. Given more time, further enhancements could be made to streamline navigation, improve content organization and accessibility for all users, and increase engagement. Overall, the NASA website redesign project was a success and provides a better experience for science enthusiasts and researchers.
